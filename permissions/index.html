How it works

Our goal is to answer “How can this paper be self-archived in an institutional repository?”.

After years of doing permissions checking by hand, we knew to answer that we needed three things. First, to know about the specific paper, e.g where and when was it published? Second, to find and understand the policies around it, for example the publisher self-archiving policy. Third, a bunch of time to find the above, double check the policies, and figure out exact embargos, deposit statements and more.

With shareyourpaper.org permissions we aimed to automate permissions checking without cutting corners and provide a simple but complete answer to the question. However, the approach is the same.

First, we track down paper metadata from Crossref, Unpaywall, and Microsoft Academic. This can tell us when and where the paper was published, if it was OA, what it’s license is. We can also often tell where authors were affiliated or who funded the paper.

Second, we use those details to find relevant policies in our database. We handcrafted a database from the ground up to do this. It includes publisher self-archiving policies, of course. However, it also includes institutional open access policies and laws where they affect self-archiving. Each of these was designed to be fully machine readable, automatically monitored daily, and archived when there is a revision. This helps us be confident that the database is up to date.

Third, we combine paper and policy information to give answers. This means we can find the exact embargo using the published date and embargo length, complete the deposit statement using citation information, tell you if a hybrid article can be achived, set the version based on an institutional policy or law, and more. If there are multiple ways to deposit, we’ll try and choose the easiest for authors.

The final step is to provide a variety of user interfaces that make checking fast and easy.

Experts actively maintain the code and data in the open, accept contributions to both, and plan to continue adding features and policies.
